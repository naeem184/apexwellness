What is Body Fat Percentage?
Body fat percentage is the amount of fat mass divided by total body mass. Unlike BMI which only uses height and weight, body fat percentage gives you a better picture of your actual body composition. Two people with the same BMI can have very different body fat percentages.

Why Body Fat Percentage Matters More Than Weight

Your weight alone doesn't tell you much about your health. Muscle weighs more than fat. So you could be losing fat and gaining muscle, and the scale might not change much. That's why body fat percentage is a better measure of progress.

When I started my health journey, I weighed 95kg. After 6 months of walking daily and eating better, I weighed 92kg. Only 3kg lost! But my body fat had dropped from 32% to 26%. My clothes fit better. I had more energy. The scale didn't tell the full story.

The US Navy Method
The US Navy method is one of the most accurate ways to measure body fat without expensive equipment. It uses simple tape measurements and has been validated by research. While not as precise as a DEXA scan, it's great for tracking changes over time.

How to Take Accurate Measurements

  • Neck: Measure just below the Adam's apple. Keep the tape horizontal.
  • Waist: For men, measure at belly button level. For women, measure at the narrowest point.
  • Hips (women only): Measure at the widest point of your buttocks.
  • Best time to measure: First thing in the morning, before eating or drinking.
  • Don't pull the tape too tight: It should be snug but not compressing your skin.

Healthy Body Fat Ranges by Age

Body fat naturally increases with age. Here are healthy ranges for different ages:

  • Ages 20-39: Men 8-20% | Women 21-32%
  • Ages 40-59: Men 11-22% | Women 23-34%
  • Ages 60-79: Men 13-25% | Women 24-36%
A Note From My Experience: Body fat percentage is just ONE measure of health. When I started, my body fat was over 28%. Now it's around 18%. But here's what matters more: I feel better. I move better. I sleep better. Don't obsess over this number. Use it as a general guide. Focus on how your clothes fit and how you feel. That's what really counts.

What to Do After Getting Your Results

  • If body fat is in healthy range: Great job! Keep doing what you're doing.
  • If body fat is above healthy range: Start with small changes. Walk daily. Eat more vegetables. Drink water instead of soda.
  • If body fat is very low: Make sure you're eating enough for your activity level.

Remember: This is just one tool. Use it to track progress over time. Measure once a month, not every day.
